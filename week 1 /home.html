Snowboarding is a recreational and competitive activity that involves
descending a snow-covered surface while standing on a snowboard that
is almost always attached to a rider's feet. It features in the Winter Olympic Games
and Winter Paralympic Games.Snowboarding was developed in the United States, inspired
by skateboarding, sledding, surfing, and skiing. It became popular around the globe, and was
introduced as a Winter Olympic Sport at Nagano in 1998[1] and featured in the Winter Paralympics
at Sochi in 2014.
